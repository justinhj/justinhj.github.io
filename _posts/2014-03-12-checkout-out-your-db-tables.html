--- layout: post title: Checkout out your DB tables date: '2014-03-12T14:03:00.001-07:00' author: Justin tags: - mysql modified_time: '2014-03-12T14:03:50.880-07:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-4777243148323391813.post-2134789851764625828 blogger_orig_url: http://justinsboringpage.blogspot.com/2014/03/checkout-out-your-db-tables.html --- I was doing some DB work today and wanted to be able to sort all DB tables based on the date they were created. Turns out you can do some neat stuff by looking in the information_schema.tables. For example this shows all the InnoDB tables.


select `table_schema`, `table_name`, `create_time`  FROM information_schema.tables where engine = 'InnoDB' order by create_time desc  ;
